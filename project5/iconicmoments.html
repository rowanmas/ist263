Iconic Knicks Moments

Willis Reed's Game 7 (1970)

Patrick Ewing's Finger Roll (1995)

 During the 1995 playoffs, Patrick Ewing's memorable finger roll against the Indiana Pacers in Game 7 of the Eastern Conference Semifinals was a defeating moment for all Knicks fans as their hopes to progress to the NBA finals were destroyed.

Bernard King's 60-Point Game (1984)

 Bernard King's 60-point masterpiece against the New Jersey Nets in 1984 remains one of the greatest individual performances in Knicks history, showcasing his scoring prowess.

Ewing's Madison Square Garden Finale (2000)

 Patrick Ewing's emotional farewell at Madison Square Garden in 2000 marked the end of an era, as the Knicks legend bid farewell to the court where he achieved so much success.

Carmelo Anthony's 62-point Game (2014)


On January 24, 2014, Carmelo Anthony delivered a legendary performance at Madison Square Garden, scoring 62 points against the Charlotte Bobcats. Anthony's remarkable feat not only set a new Knicks franchise record but also established a new Madison Square Garden single-game scoring record.

Linsanity (2012)


Jeremy Lin's unexpected emergence in 2012, known as "Linsanity," saw the undrafted guard leading the Knicks to a remarkable winning streak. Lin's electrifying performances captivated fans worldwide and became a cultural phenomenon.

Clyde Frazier's Game 7 Triple-Double (1970)


Walt Frazier's triple-double in Game 7 of the 1970 NBA Finals solidified his status as a Knicks legend, contributing significantly to the team's historic championship victory.
